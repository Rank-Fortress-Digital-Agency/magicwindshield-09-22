How To Handle a Windshield Crack


Let's say your windshield has just been damaged by a rock flung up by the car in front of you. Before you panic, know there are ways to fix this type of problem. While driving around with a cracked windshield is not advisable, you can take cautionary measures until you can get it professionally repaired. A windshield crack can be significant pain on the road. Not only is it unsightly, but it can also obstruct your view and make driving dangerous. Luckily, you can do a few things to deal with a windshield crack until you can get it repaired.

What To Do If Your Windshield Cracks While Driving

If you're driving while your windshield cracks, staying calm is essential. Pull over to a safe area, turn on your hazard lights, and call for help. You may want to temporarily cover the crack with clear tape or a piece of cardboard to prevent further damage.

If the crack is small, you may be able to repair it with a DIY kit. These kits typically come with a resin that you can inject into the gap to fill it and prevent it from spreading. If you choose to do this, follow the instructions carefully, as improper use can worsen the damage. This is only a temporary fix, and the crack will still need to be professionally repaired.

What To Do If You Get A Large Windshield Crack While Driving


If you get a large windshield crack while driving, you must immediately pull over and call for help. Driving with a cracked windshield is dangerous, as the glass could shatter and cause severe injury. Once in a safe area, turn on your hazard lights and call a tow truck or roadside assistance to take your car to a nearby repair shop.

Depending on the crack's size, the windshield will need to be replaced entirely at the repair shop. This is more costly and time-consuming than repairing minor damage, but it's crucial to ensure your safety on the road.

What To Do When You Need A Windshield Replacement


Auto glass technicians determine whether a windshield needs to be repaired or replaced based on the crack's size, depth, and location. If the damage is in your line of sight or is over three inches, the windshield will need to be replaced.

If your windshield needs to be replaced, the technician will remove the old windshield and install a new one. The process usually takes about an hour, and you'll be able to drive your car as soon as the new windshield is installed.

How To Choose The Best Auto Glass Shop For Windshield Replacements

Selecting an experienced and reputable auto glass shop is vital when replacing your windshield. Ask friends or family for recommendations or look for online reviews. Make sure the shop uses high-quality or OEM materials and has a good reputation for customer service. You can even check with your car insurance to find the insurance preferred auto glass shops in your area.

Replacing your windshield can be a hassle, but doing it as soon as possible is necessary to ensure your safety on the road. With some research, you can find a reputable shop that will do the job quickly and efficiently.

How To Pay For A Windshield Repair Or Replacement


The cost of windshield repairs or replacement will vary depending on the damage's severity and the car's make and model. Many insurance companies will cover at least a portion of the cost, so it's worth checking with your provider to see if they offer any coverage.

If you need to pay for the repairs or replacement out of pocket, you can expect to pay anywhere from $100 to $400 for a small crack and upwards of $1,000 for a significant break or replacement.

How To Prevent Windshield Cracks

Windshields are susceptible to damage from rocks, debris, and even extreme temperature changes. To help prevent cracks, it's essential to regularly inspect your windshield for any damage and have it repaired as soon as possible. It's also helpful to have your windshield checked out when you get small chips on your windshield.

You can help prevent damage by avoiding driving on rough roads and keeping your car in a garage or covered parking spot when possible. These precautions can help keep your windshield in good condition and avoid costly repairs or replacements.

Contact Your Local Auto Glass Shop For Any Questions

If you have questions about your windshield damage, don't hesitate to reach out to a local auto glass shop, like Magic Windshield. The professionals there can help you determine the best course of action and provide a repair or replacement quote.

Taking care of your windshield is essential for maintaining a safe driving experience. Be sure to take the necessary precautions and contact an auto glass shop at the first sign of damage.
